SPECIES: other

FEEDING:
- Research your specific pet's dietary needs carefully, as they vary widely.
- Rabbits need unlimited hay, fresh vegetables, and limited pellets daily.
- Guinea pigs need hay, fresh vegetables rich in vitamin C, and pellets.
- Birds need species-appropriate seeds, pellets, and fresh fruits/vegetables.
- Small mammals like hamsters and gerbils need a balanced seed and pellet mix.
- Always provide fresh, clean water daily.
- Avoid giving pets human food unless confirmed safe for their species.

EXERCISE:
- Rabbits need at least 3 hours of exercise outside their enclosure daily.
- Guinea pigs need a large enclosure and daily floor time for exploration.
- Birds need daily out-of-cage time and mental stimulation.
- Hamsters are nocturnal and need a wheel for nightly exercise.
- Research the specific exercise needs for your pet's species and age.

GROOMING:
- Rabbits groom themselves but need brushing during shedding season.
- Guinea pigs need regular nail trims and brushing depending on coat type.
- Birds may need baths or misting 2-3 times per week.
- Check your pet regularly for signs of overgrown nails, fur matting, or skin issues.
- Most small pets should not be bathed unless specifically needed.

HEALTH:
- Find a vet experienced with exotic or small animals for your specific pet.
- Annual wellness checkups are recommended for most pets.
- Watch for changes in eating, drinking, activity level, or droppings — these signal health issues.
- Maintain a clean habitat to prevent bacterial and parasitic infections.
- Research species-specific health risks and vaccinations if applicable.

ENRICHMENT:
- All pets benefit from mental stimulation and environmental enrichment.
- Provide hiding spots, tunnels, and climbing structures appropriate for your pet.
- Rotate toys and enrichment items to maintain interest.
- Social species (guinea pigs, rabbits, birds) may need companionship from their own kind.
- Research natural behaviors for your pet's species and try to encourage them in captivity.
