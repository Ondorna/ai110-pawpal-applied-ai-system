SPECIES: dog

FEEDING:
- Adult dogs should be fed 2 times per day, typically morning and evening.
- Puppies under 6 months need 3-4 small meals per day.
- Senior dogs (7+ years) may need smaller, more frequent meals.
- Always provide fresh water at all times.
- Avoid feeding dogs chocolate, grapes, onions, garlic, and xylitol — these are toxic.

EXERCISE:
- Most adult dogs need 30-60 minutes of exercise per day.
- High-energy breeds (Labrador, Border Collie, Husky) need 1-2 hours per day.
- Small or senior dogs may only need 20-30 minutes of gentle walking.
- Puppies should have short, frequent play sessions (5 minutes per month of age).
- Avoid intense exercise right after meals to prevent bloat.

GROOMING:
- Short-haired dogs need brushing once a week.
- Long-haired dogs need brushing 3-4 times per week to prevent matting.
- Bathe dogs every 4-6 weeks, or when dirty.
- Trim nails every 3-4 weeks to prevent overgrowth.
- Check and clean ears weekly to prevent infections.
- Brush teeth 2-3 times per week to prevent dental disease.

HEALTH:
- Dogs should visit the vet at least once a year for a wellness checkup.
- Puppies need a series of vaccinations starting at 6-8 weeks.
- Monthly heartworm prevention medication is strongly recommended.
- Flea and tick prevention should be applied monthly.
- Spaying or neutering is recommended at 6 months unless breeding.
- Watch for signs of illness: lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea, loss of appetite.

ENRICHMENT:
- Dogs need mental stimulation daily — puzzle toys, training sessions, or sniff walks.
- Socialization with other dogs and people is important, especially for puppies.
- Training sessions of 5-15 minutes per day help keep dogs mentally sharp.
- Rotate toys regularly to keep dogs engaged and prevent boredom.
